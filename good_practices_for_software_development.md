# GOOD PRACTICES FOR SOFTWARE DEVELOPMENT

### 1. Which point(s) were new to you?
- The idea of over-communicating proactively instead of waiting for issues to escalate
- Using group channels over private DMs to improve transparency and shared context
- The importance of documenting requirements and sharing them early for feedback
- How the quality of a question directly affects the quality of help received
- Using tools like Loom, GitHub Gists, and sandbox environments to ask better questions
- Being mindful of other team members’ time and availability while communicating
- Treating focus and attention as a skill that can be trained through deep work practices

### 2. Which area do you think you need to improve on? What are your ideas to make progress in that area?
- **Over-communication:** I need to give updates more frequently instead of assuming things are understood
- **Asking better questions:** I plan to clearly explain the problem, what I tried, and the exact issue
- **Time and focus management:** I will reduce distractions and practice deep work during work hours
- **Early documentation:** I will write down requirements and share them for confirmation
- **Being more visible in communication:** I will keep my camera on and participate actively in meetings
- **Respecting others’ time:** I will consolidate questions and choose the right communication medium
- **Consistency in work habits:** I will track my time and maintain a healthy routine for better productivity
