## 1. What are the steps/strategies to do Active Listening? 

- Give full, undivided attention to the speaker.
- Use positive body language like eye contact and nodding.
- Avoid interrupting and let the speaker finish.
- Reflect or paraphrase to confirm understanding.
- Ask clarifying questions to explore the message deeper.
- Respond thoughtfully, showing empathy and respect.

## 2. According to Fisher's model, what are the key points of Reflective Listening?

- Listen carefully and try to understand the speaker’s real meaning.
- Repeat or restate what the speaker said to show that you understood correctly.
- Notice the speaker’s feelings and reflect them back.
- Ask questions to make the message clearer.
- Avoid judging, arguing, or giving quick solutions.
- Make the speaker feel heard and respected.
- Pay attention to both words and body language.
- Give the speaker enough time and space to express themselves fully.

## 3. What are the obstacles in your listening process?

- Getting distracted by phones, noises, or other activities.
- Thinking about what you will say next instead of listening.
- Being emotionally upset, stressed, or tired.
- Losing interest because the topic feels boring or long.
- Not paying attention to the speaker’s tone and body language.

## 4. What can you do to improve your listening?

- Stay focused on the speaker and remove distractions.
- Maintain eye contact and show interest with your body language.
- Avoid interrupting and let the speaker finish fully.
- Ask questions when something is unclear.
- Paraphrase or repeat key points to confirm understanding.
- Control emotional reactions and stay calm.
- Practice patience, especially in long or difficult conversations.

## 5. When do you switch to Passive communication style in your day to day life?
- When you want to avoid conflict or don’t want to upset someone.
- When you feel nervous, shy, or unsure about your opinion.
- When the other person is aggressive or dominating.
- When you don’t think your ideas will be accepted or valued.
- When you want to keep peace in the situation rather than argue.

## 6. When do you switch into Aggressive communication styles in your day to day life?
- When you feel angry, frustrated, or stressed.
- When someone disagrees with you and you want to “win” the argument.
- When you feel disrespected or not listened to.
- When you are in a hurry and want things done your way.
- When you believe your opinion is the only correct one and ignore others’ views.

## 7. When do you switch into Passive Aggressive communication styles in your day to day life?
- When you feel hurt or ignored but don’t know how to express it directly.
- When you want to avoid open conflict but still want your feelings to be noticed.
- When you feel powerless or not comfortable speaking openly.
- When you want to protect the relationship but are still upset inside.
- When you hope the other person will understand your feelings without a direct confrontation.

## 8. How can you make your communication assertive? What would be a few steps you can apply in your own life? 
- Express your thoughts clearly and respectfully — say what you feel or need without being harsh or silent.
- Use calm tone and confident body language — maintain eye contact, speak slowly, and stay relaxed.
- Set healthy boundaries — politely say “no” or explain what you can and cannot do.
